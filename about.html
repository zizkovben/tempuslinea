EDITORIAL & EVIDENCE POLICY

THE ATLANTIS TEST

A theory earns a Tempus Linea entry if it has:

  • A named proponent
  • A consistent geographical or temporal claim
  • Engagement with existing literature
  • Internal logical consistency

NO GATEKEEPING

Mainstream archaeology and alternative theories receive identical structural treatment. Community plausibility scores — not editors — determine how theories are ranked. A theory cited by 10,000 users may outrank one cited by one paper.

PRE-10,800 BCE RULE

All civilizations with a start date before 10,800 BCE are classified debated or theorized by default. This is the Younger Dryas boundary — no confirmed continuous civilisation predates it.

CITATION ETHICS

Researchers are cited sources, not platform participants, unless they hold a verified account. Claims are always attributed to the specific book, paper, or documented source — never attributed without reference.
